THE MONASTERY

A space beyond the ordinary

What It Is

A monastery. A refugee camp. A laboratory and research hub.

A refuge and container for those seeking depth. A classroom to learn and share unique skills and practices.

A home for wild seekers and path-forgers—artists, poets, philosophers—who don't fit neatly inside a single tradition. A home for those who have rejected their culture and chosen to walk their own path.

The Vision

A space where deep meditation meets depth psychology, group work, and art. A place for those who have gone so deeply into one thing that they've emerged into everything again.

It's rare to find truly intelligent humans who are also on the path. The world is full of intelligence, but often without a mystical dimension. Some communities (for example, in Bali) overflow with mysticism that can be ungrounded or prone to spiritual bypassing. Many artists touch the profound and live close to poetry and depth, yet may lack intellectual cultivation or an understanding of their spirituality. Likewise, many artists, mystics, and intellectuals carry unresolved psychological issues or insecure attachment patterns.

The Monastery exists at the meeting point of four pillars: intellect, art, relating, and mysticism.

The Four Pillars

Art

Art circumambulates the unsymbolizable. It draws a dotted line around the profound and takes us as close as images and words allow.

Mysticism

Direct experience of the divine through practices that cultivate altered states and embodied understanding.

Intellect

Skepticism, clarity, insight—the ability to conceptualize your own path. A tool to be used wisely, not mistaken for the answer.

Relating

Many psychological difficulties are relational in nature. We are social beings; how we relate to others is central to any authentic path.

At The Monastery, we cultivate all four.

How We Practice

Art

Art is encouraged—especially forms that shift consciousness (e.g., free associative art). Poetry and metaphor are foundational and infused across mediums. We learn to "follow the metaphor." This may be integrated with Jungian practices like active imagination or understood through Bionian reverie, deepening access to the subconscious with more clarity.

Mysticism

The Monastery is a research lab for mystical practice. Commit your life to Nei Gong, Vipassana, or another lineage? Do it. Explore altered states through breathwork or shamanic drumming? Go for it. Different practices serve different purposes and affect the body and energy system in distinct ways. We respect each other's paths and learn from them. Transformation is our measure of efficacy.

Relating

We cultivate a culture of conscious relating: Authentic Relating, Transformational Connection, Circling, psychodrama, and more. These practices weave the social fabric, provide accountability, and create the container for deep research into every other area.

Intellect

We teach, learn, question, and critique—grounded in curiosity, care, and authentic relating. We notice when intellectual conversation is driven by emotional undertones rather than inquiry. We study and research our practices to understand what we are actually doing, and why.

Ways of Living

Ecological and embodied ethics.

Situated in nature with permaculture-based food systems.

Encouragement of foraging and cultivation of herbs and medicinal plants, alongside traditional medicine practices.

On Play and Sexuality

Play is encouraged. Certain sexual practices are welcomed when they are consciously explored for growth and integration—not for hedonism. Couples are, of course, free to have sex as they wish. The emphasis is on awareness, consent, and development rather than indulgence.

Governance and Culture

Non-hierarchical in ethic, with practical differences in decision-making power where relevant (e.g., financial investment).

The Monastery is a non-profit organization

Group practices and circling function as social accountability.

Members may vote to expel an individual if their behavior significantly disrupts the mission or compromises the container

Curation Over Inclusion

The Monastery is not designed to be universally inclusive. It is intentionally curated.

Who Should Apply

You don't need to be a professional artist, a monk, or a philosopher. What matters is that you recognize the importance of all four pillars and arrive with curiosity and a desire to cultivate them.

This is not primarily a training institution, its a transformative social container. Arriving with a sense of what you wish to develop is recommended

Prerequisite: Some experience with relating practices (e.g., Transformational Connection, Circling, Authentic Relating) is required.
